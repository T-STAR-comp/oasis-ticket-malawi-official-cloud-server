PART A — TERMS OF SERVICE
================================================================================

1. ACCEPTANCE
By creating an account, purchasing a ticket, applying as an organizer/partner, or
otherwise using Ticket Malawi (the "Platform"), you agree to these Terms of Service
and our Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, do not use the Platform.

Organizer/partner applicants must additionally accept organizer-specific obligations
below. Account creation requires explicit acceptance via checkbox before signup
completes.

2. ABOUT THE PLATFORM
2.1 Ticket Malawi was first introduced in 2024 and re-introduced in 2026 under
    Oasis Technology and Capital. The platform is now a product of Oasis Tech
    Capital.
2.2 Ticket Malawi is a marketplace for event tickets and intercity travel seats
    in Malawi. We strive to provide competent ticketing services as digital
    technology use rises across the country. We connect customers with independent
    organizers and operators.
2.3 Ticket Malawi facilitates listing, payment, ticket issuance, and settlement
    but is not the event host or transport operator unless expressly stated.

3. ACCOUNTS
3.1 Customers must provide accurate registration details and keep credentials secure.
3.2 Organizers are approved through a partner application process and receive a
    separate organizer dashboard upon approval.
3.3 You must be at least 18 years old to create an account.
3.4 One person must not maintain multiple accounts to evade suspension, bans, or
    reporting restrictions.

4. PURCHASES & TICKETS
4.1 Prices are shown in Malawi Kwacha (MWK). A platform service fee may apply at
    checkout.
4.2 Confirmed purchases issue digital tickets with a unique reference and QR code
    where applicable.
4.3 Tickets may become inactive when an event or travel date has passed by one
    calendar day, when used at entry, when expired under platform rules, when an
    organizer cancels the listing (Section 8.6), or when affected by organizer
    moderation actions described in Section 7.
4.4 Ticket transfers between customer accounts are permitted only through the
    Platform's official share feature.

5. ORGANIZER & PARTNER OBLIGATIONS
5.1 Organizers must provide truthful listing information (dates, venues, capacity,
    pricing, routes, and operator details).
5.2 Organizers must honour valid tickets and comply with applicable laws.
5.3 Organizers must not list fraudulent, misleading, unsafe, illegal, or explicit
    content. Listings containing prohibited language may result in immediate
    suspension.
5.4 Settlement follows our T+1 policy: earnings become withdrawable after the
    calendar day following payment completion, subject to chargebacks, refunds,
    moderation holds, and cancellation holds (Section 8.6).
5.5 Organizers accept commission and fee structures communicated during onboarding
    and in the finance dashboard.

6. REPORTING — RULES FOR USERS
6.1 Signed-in customers may report an organizer from a listing page using approved
    report reasons only:
    - Fraudulent or fake listing
    - Misleading event or travel information
    - No-show or unfair cancellation
    - Harassment or abuse
    - Unsafe or illegal content
    - Payment or refund issue
    - Spam or scam activity
    - Poor service or unprofessional conduct
    - Other (requires a detailed description of at least 20 characters)
6.2 Reports must be truthful and submitted in good faith. Knowingly false or
    abusive reports may result in action against the reporting account.
6.3 You may not report your own organizer account.
6.4 Duplicate reports of the same reason against the same organizer within 24
    hours are not accepted.
6.5 Reports are confidential to the extent permitted by law. We do not disclose
    reporter identity to the subject organizer during initial review, except where
    required by law or necessary to resolve a specific dispute with your
    participation.

7. MODERATION, FLAGS, SUSPENSION & BANS
7.1 Automated review: When an organizer receives 200 or more user reports (in total
    or for a single report category), the Platform may:
    (a) Place a temporary public flag on the organizer profile and listings;
    (b) Automatically suspend the organizer for seven (7) days.
7.2 Flagged organizers remain visible with a flag indicator until an administrator
    reviews the matter. Suspended or banned organizers' listings are removed from
    public view.
7.3 Suspension triggers:
    - Mass reporting threshold (Section 7.1);
    - Explicit or prohibited language in listings;
    - Administrator action;
    - Content or conduct violations.
7.4 During suspension or ban:
    - The organizer may sign in but the dashboard is read-only except logout and,
      for suspensions, submitting an appeal;
    - Public listings are hidden;
    - Customers with active tickets see tickets greyed out with a hold message;
    - Affected customers and the organizer receive email notification.
7.5 Appeals: Suspended organizers may submit one appeal at a time with a minimum
    30-character explanation. Administrators may approve or reject appeals. Approval
    may restore approved status where appropriate.
7.6 Permanent ban: Administrators may permanently ban an organizer after review,
    including for severe or repeated violations. Effects include:
    - All non-draft listings taken down;
    - Organizer account permanently restricted;
    - Eligible active tickets reviewed for refund under Section 8.
7.7 Ticket Malawi's decisions on moderation are exercised in good faith to protect
    users. They do not replace law-enforcement or court processes where those apply.

8. REFUNDS — PERMANENT BAN AND LISTING CANCELLATION
8.1 When an organizer is permanently banned, the Platform reviews active tickets
    from that organizer.
8.2 Where payment has completed and T+1 settlement has occurred for the underlying
    order, customers may receive a 90% refund of the amount paid on the affected
    ticket.
8.3 The remaining 10% covers payment processing and Ticket Malawi's convenience fee,
    allocated between processing costs and platform fee as determined by our refund
    policy.
8.4 Where settlement has not completed, refunds may be deferred or skipped until
    settlement criteria are met; customers are notified by email.
8.5 Refunds are processed through our payment partners where technically available;
    recorded refund status is visible in our systems.
8.6 Listing cancellation: When an organizer cancels an event or travel listing
    through the Platform's cancel action:
    (a) The listing is marked cancelled and removed from sale;
    (b) All active tickets for that listing become invalid;
    (c) Eligible ticket holders are notified by email;
    (d) Where payment has completed and T+1 settlement has occurred, customers
        receive a 90% refund of the amount paid on the affected ticket;
    (e) The remaining 10% covers payment processing and Ticket Malawi's convenience
        fee, as with permanent-ban refunds (Sections 8.2–8.3);
    (f) Where settlement has not completed, refunds are deferred until T+1 criteria
        are met; customers receive email notice of cancellation and pending refund;
    (g) All funds associated with the cancelled listing (settled and unsettled
        organizer earnings from that listing) are held and are not available for
        organizer payout;
    (h) The organizer receives email confirmation of cancellation, refund summary,
        and funds held.
8.7 Cancellation refunds and permanent-ban refunds follow the same 90%/10% split.
    Organizers may not circumvent cancellation holds by changing listing status
    outside the official cancel workflow.
8.8 Refund debt and recovery from future sales:
    (a) If an organizer has withdrawn earnings and subsequently cancels a listing
        or is permanently banned, customer refunds may exceed funds held from the
        affected listing(s). The shortfall is recorded as refund debt on the
        organizer account.
    (b) Funds held from a cancelled listing are applied toward customer refunds
        before refund debt is calculated.
    (c) While refund debt remains outstanding, organizer withdrawals are blocked.
    (d) Settled earnings from new ticket sales on the organizer's active listings
        are applied to outstanding customer refunds until refund debt is fully
        cleared. Only after every affected refund has been paid or recovered
        will the organizer have a positive withdrawable balance again.
    (e) This recovery applies across all of the organizer's listings until the
        outstanding refund debt reaches zero.

9. PROHIBITED CONDUCT
Users and organizers must not: commit fraud; circumvent security; scrape the
Platform; harass staff or users; manipulate reporting; impersonate others; upload
malware; or use the Platform for unlawful purposes.

10. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

11. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

indirect losses, organizer no-shows, third-party transport delays, or events beyond
our reasonable control. Our aggregate liability relating to a transaction is limited

12. CHANGES
We may update these Terms. Material changes will be reflected in the version date.
Continued use after notice constitutes acceptance where permitted by law.

13. GOVERNING LAW
These Terms are governed by the laws of the Republic of Malawi. Disputes are

14. CONTACT

PART B — PRIVACY POLICY (MALAWI DATA PROTECTION ACT 2024)
================================================================================

1. INTRODUCTION
Ticket Malawi respects your privacy. This Policy explains how we collect, use,
store, and protect personal data in compliance with the Malawi Data Protection Act,
2024 ("the Act") and regulations issued by the Malawi Communications Regulatory
Authority (MACRA) and the Data Protection Authority.

We are committed to lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data
minimisation, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, and confidentiality.

2. ROLES
Ticket Malawi / Oasis acts as a data controller for account, transaction, and
moderation data. Payment processing may involve data processors (e.g. PayChangu)
acting under contract.

3. DATA WE COLLECT
3.1 Account data: name, email, phone, national ID (optional), username, password
    (hashed), role, verification status.
3.2 Transaction data: orders, ticket references, payment method, amounts, seats.
3.3 Organizer data: company details, payout bank information, listings, buyer
    manifests.
3.4 Moderation data: reports, flags, appeals, suspension records, verification
    scans.
3.5 Technical data: logs, IP addresses, device/browser information for security.
3.6 Communications: emails we send (verification, reminders, moderation notices).

4. LAWFUL BASES FOR PROCESSING
We process personal data on one or more of the following bases under the Act:
- Contract: to provide ticketing, payments, and organizer services you request;
- Consent: marketing only where you opt in (see Section 6);
- Legal obligation: fraud prevention, regulatory requests, tax/record-keeping;
- Legitimate interests: platform security, abuse prevention, and service improvement
  balanced against your rights.

5. HOW WE USE DATA — NO ADVERTISING USE
5.1 We use your data to operate the Platform: authentication, ticket issuance,
    payments, settlement, customer support, event reminders, and moderation.
5.2 WE DO NOT SELL YOUR PERSONAL DATA.
5.3 WE DO NOT USE CUSTOMER PERSONAL DATA FOR THIRD-PARTY ADVERTISING OR BEHAVIOURAL
    ADVERTISING PROFILING.
5.4 Service-related emails (verification, receipts, reminders, security, moderation
    updates) are not marketing and may be sent as part of the service.
5.5 Promotional communications, if any in future, will require separate opt-in
    consent where required by law.

6. REPORTING DATA
6.1 When you submit a report, we store your user ID, the organizer reported,
    optional listing ID, reason, details, and timestamp.
6.2 This data is used solely for trust & safety review, automated threshold checks,
    administrator investigation, and legal compliance.
6.3 Report data is retained for the period necessary to resolve complaints and
    defend legal claims, then deleted or anonymised per our retention schedule.

7. SHARING & PROCESSORS
We share data only where necessary with:
- Payment providers (PayChangu) to process payments and payouts;
- Email delivery services for transactional messages;
- Infrastructure providers hosting our systems under confidentiality obligations;
- Authorities when required by Malawian law or valid legal process.

8. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS
Where data is processed outside Malawi, we implement appropriate safeguards as
required by the Act (e.g. contractual clauses, adequacy assessments).

9. RETENTION
We retain data only as long as necessary for the purposes collected, including:
- Active accounts: duration of relationship plus reasonable period;
- Financial records: as required for settlement, tax, and audit;
- Moderation records: to enforce decisions and prevent repeat abuse;
- Inactive accounts: deleted or anonymised per internal schedules.

10. YOUR RIGHTS UNDER THE ACT
Subject to exceptions in the Act, you may have the right to:
- Access your personal data;
- Rectify inaccurate data;
- Erase data in certain circumstances;
- Restrict or object to certain processing;
- Data portability where technically feasible;
- Withdraw consent where processing is consent-based;
- Lodge a complaint with the Data Protection Authority (info@dpa.mw).

To exercise rights, contact us using Platform support channels. We respond within
timeframes required by law.

11. SECURITY
We implement technical and organisational measures including access controls,
encrypted connections (HTTPS), hashed passwords, and restricted staff access.
No method of transmission is 100% secure; report suspected compromise promptly.

12. DATA BREACHES
If a personal data breach poses risk to your rights, we will notify MACRA / the
Data Protection Authority and affected individuals as required by the Act,
typically within 72 hours of becoming aware where feasible.

13. CHILDREN
The Platform is not directed at children under 18. We do not knowingly collect
children's data without parental authority.

14. COOKIES & LOCAL STORAGE
We use essential cookies and local storage (e.g. authentication tokens) for login
and session management. Non-essential analytics cookies, if introduced, will be
disclosed with consent options where required.

15. CHANGES TO THIS POLICY
We update this Policy when practices or law change. The version date at the top
indicates the current edition. Material changes will be communicated on the
Platform.

16. CONTACT
Privacy enquiries: via Ticket Malawi support channels published on the website.
Data Protection Authority: info@dpa.mw | www.dpa.mw
